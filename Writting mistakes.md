## 📅 2026-03-05

### Mistake 1 · Adjective alone cannot describe occupation after *zijn*

> [!warning] ❌ Wrong
> "Ik ben administratief."

> [!success] ✅ Correct
> "Ik doe **administratief werk**."
> or: "Ik ben een **administratief medewerker**."

> [!note] 📌 Rule
> In Dutch, adjectives describing a job or type of work **must be followed by a noun** — they cannot stand alone after *zijn* (to be).
> - ❌ *Ik ben administratief.* → incomplete, like saying "I am administrative"
> - ✅ *Ik doe administratief **werk**.* → I do administrative work

- [x] Mastered

### Mistake 2 · Negation: *niet* vs. *geen*

> [!warning] ❌ Wrong
> "Ik ben 24 jaar en **geen** getrouwd."

> [!success] ✅ Correct
> "Ik ben 24 jaar en **niet** getrouwd."

> [!note] 📌 Rule
> | Word | Negates | Example |
> |------|---------|---------|
> | **geen** | a noun (= not a / no) | *Ik heb geen auto.* |
> | **niet** | a verb, adjective, or adverb | *Ik ben niet getrouwd.* |
>
> *getrouwd* is an adjective → use **niet**

- [x] Mastered

---

### Mistake 3 · Missing verb + wrong preposition

> [!warning] ❌ Wrong
> "Ik boodschappen **in** de markt."

> [!success] ✅ Correct
> "Ik **doe** boodschappen **op** de markt."

> [!note] 📌 Rule
> - Fixed phrase: **boodschappen doen** (to do grocery shopping) — the verb *doen* is mandatory, never drop it
> - At the market = **op** de markt (open-air/outdoor space)
> - *in* is used for enclosed spaces: *in de supermarkt*, *in de winkel*

- [ ] Mastered
